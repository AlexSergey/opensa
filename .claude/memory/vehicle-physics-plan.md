Plan: `.claude/plans/018-vehicle-physics.md`. **User directive:** the car must be a real **dynamic physical body** whose collision **is the model's COL** (reused later for damage — hit hood → hood damaged; COL is unique per car, must not be replaced), with real **gravity** + suspension, "like GTA SA". The arcade-kinematic drive ([[vehicle-driving-plan]] iters 1–3) is the wrong base and gets replaced.

**Approach = Rapier `DynamicRayCastVehicleController`** (maps onto GTA's model): chassis = dynamic rigid body (mass from handling.cfg); **chassis collider = convex hull of the COL vertices** (dynamic bodies can't collide with the static world trimesh using a concave trimesh — two trimeshes generate no contacts in Rapier → car falls through; convex hull is the same model geometry, just convex; full COL kept for damage); 4 **raycast wheels** at `wheel_*_dummy` (suspension/radius from handling) → gravity rests the car on wheels (no hover/settle/snag hacks needed); `setWheelEngineForce`/brake/`setWheelSteering` from handling.cfg + WSAD; `updateVehicle(dt)` each fixed step; render copies chassis transform → car object + wheel transforms → rig. API verified present (world.createVehicleController, addWheel, etc.).

**Removes** the `drive`/`moveCar`/`stopping`/kinematic-box/sensor/`createKinematicVehicle`/`groundHeight`/settle machinery from EnterVehicleSystem. Get-in/out kept but the seated player rides a **dynamic** chassis (sync to a chassis seat point; player collider off while seated).

**Iter 1 tuning gotchas (resolved, verified offline via a throwaway vitest that sims a car on a static box with admiral's real geometry):**
- The chassis hull bottom (COL minZ) sits only ~0.13 m (admiral) / 0.26 m (camper) above the wheel contact. If suspension equilibrium compression exceeds that margin, the hull sinks into the road / tips. → keep suspension stiff enough that compression is small. Final: stiffness 120, rest 0.15, travel 0.25, maxForce 40000, damping comp 4.4 / relax 2.3 (Bullet-standard), frictionSlip 10.5. Admiral then rests with hull ~0.12 m clear.
- **`getLinvel` on a raycast-vehicle body returns a PHANTOM velocity** (~0.95 vz on flat ground) that `updateVehicle` writes each frame — the body does NOT actually move (zOsc≈0). Don't use it (nor likely `currentVehicleSpeed`) for wheel roll: on a tilted trimesh the phantom has a horizontal component that spins parked wheels. → VehiclePhysicsSystem now rolls wheels from the car's **real planar displacement** frame-to-frame (≈0 at rest), deadzone 0.1 m/s.

**Iter 1 also: chassis collider = COL spheres+boxes (NOT a single convex hull).** A single hull bridges over the concave hood (Tommy floated ~25cm above it). admiral COL = 23 spheres + trimesh, 0 boxes; camper = 20 spheres. `addVehicleHull` builds ball/cuboid colliders from the COL spheres/boxes (spreading mass by density so body mass = handling.mass), skips the trimesh (kept for damage), convex-hull fallback only if no primitives. Spheres hug the contour → standing on hood/roof is correct. Confirmed in browser.

**ENTRY/EXIT POP — RESOLVED (user confirmed "excellent").** Long hunt. The pop was the **kinematic player body shoving the dynamic car while sliding into/out of the seat**. Critical finding: NO collider-state trick suppresses the kinematic→dynamic push IN-GAME — `setColliderEnabled(false)`, collision groups (`ignoreVehicles`), and `setColliderSensor(true)` ALL work in isolated vitest sims (push→0) but ALL failed in the running game (camper still popped). Root unknown (some game-loop/integration difference), do NOT trust these to stop a kinematic body pushing a dynamic one in-game. The body-stays-out approach (moveBody:false, never teleport the body into the car) fixed the pop but broke render/exit/controls (body↔render divergence) — reverted. **Final fix: PIN the car during the getin/getout slides** — `EnterVehicleSystem.storeHold` snapshots the parked car transform at startGetin/startGetout; `advanceGetin`/`advanceGetout` call `PhysicsWorld.holdBody(body, pos, quat)` each (fixed-step) frame, which sets translation+rotation and zeroes lin/ang velocity. A pinned car can't be shoved. Seated driving is NOT pinned (untouched). Also: coast-to-stop before exit (phase 'stopping'), seated rider + slides run on the fixed step, follow camera tracks the car object while seated, ENGINE_RAMP 0.2, BRAKE_FORCE 480 (Rapier brake is small-scale not Newtons!), setCanSleep(false) (sleeping chassis sank→launch pop), low engine-launch.
